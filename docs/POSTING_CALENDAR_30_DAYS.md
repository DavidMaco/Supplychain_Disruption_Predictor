# 30-Day Posting Calendar

This calendar turns the existing content pack into a practical publishing sequence.
It is designed to do three things:

- introduce the project clearly
- build credibility over time
- convert attention into conversation, profile visits, portfolio traffic, and recruiter interest

## How to use this calendar

- Post on LinkedIn 2 to 3 times per week using the longer drafts.
- Publish 1 Medium story per week using the matching topic from the content pack.
- Use X more frequently for shorter observations, reposts, and threaded versions.
- Reuse the same weekly theme across all three platforms so your message compounds instead of scattering.

## Recommended voice

- practical
- thoughtful
- analytical but accessible
- honest about tradeoffs
- confident without overselling

## Weekly theme structure

### Week 1

Theme: The business problem and why it matters

### Week 2

Theme: How the project works and the logic behind it

### Week 3

Theme: Decisions, business value, and credibility

### Week 4

Theme: Reflection, lessons, and professional positioning

## Best posting order for the 8 main themes

Use this order for the major content themes because it moves from problem to solution to credibility:

1. The business problem this project solves
2. The questions a procurement team can answer with it
3. How the project works in plain language
4. The logic behind the risk score and the model
5. What actions a business can take from the output
6. The importance of data quality and the join bug
7. Why honest documentation and measured claims matter
8. What this project demonstrates overall

## CTA bank

Use these without repeating the same one every time.

- How does your team currently spot supplier risk early?
- Which of these signals would matter most in your environment?
- Would your team trust a simple score, a model output, or both?
- What would you do first with a high-risk order alert?
- Have you seen a data quality issue distort business insight before?
- How do you separate scenario estimates from measured results in your work?
- Which part of this project would you want to see expanded next?

## Platform-specific notes

### LinkedIn

- Use stronger hooks in the first 2 lines.
- Keep 1 clear lesson per post.
- End with a question to drive comments.

### Medium

- Expand one idea fully instead of trying to cover everything.
- Use subheadings and plain language.
- Include one concrete example or analogy in each story.

### X

- Keep each post tightly focused.
- Use threads for process, lessons, or reflections.
- Link back to LinkedIn or the repo when useful.

## Recommended publishing cadence

- LinkedIn: Monday, Thursday, Saturday
- Medium: Wednesday or Sunday
- X: Tuesday, Friday, and recap posts on weekends

That cadence keeps the project visible without making every day feel repetitive.
